29 /***********************************************************************
30 * The structure LUF describes sparse LU-factorization.
31 *
32 * The LU-factorization has the following format:
33 *
34 * A = F * V = P * L * U * Q, (1)
35 *
36 * F = P * L * P', (2)
37 *
38 * V = P * U * Q, (3)
39 *
40 * where A is a given (unsymmetric) square matrix, F and V are matrix
41 * factors actually computed, L is a lower triangular matrix with unity
42 * diagonal, U is an upper triangular matrix, P and Q are permutation
43 * matrices, P' is a matrix transposed to P. All the matrices have the
44 * same order n.
45 *
46 * Matrices F and V are stored in both row- and column-wise sparse
47 * formats in the associated sparse vector area (SVA). Unity diagonal
48 * elements of matrix F are not stored. Pivot elements of matrix V
49 * (which correspond to diagonal elements of matrix U) are stored in
50 * a separate ordinary array.
51 *
52 * Permutation matrices P and Q are stored in ordinary arrays in both
53 * row- and column-like formats.
54 *
55 * Matrices L and U are completely defined by matrices F, V, P, and Q,
56 * and therefore not stored explicitly. */
